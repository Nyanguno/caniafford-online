How the Car Affordability Calculator Works

Our car affordability calculator uses the industry-standard 20/4/10 rule to determine how much car you can afford. This rule recommends putting down at least 20% as a down payment, financing for no more than 4 years, and keeping your total monthly vehicle expenses under 10% of your gross monthly income.

The calculator considers your annual income, down payment, interest rate, loan term, and insurance costs to provide you with a realistic estimate of your car buying budget.

💡 The 20% Down Payment

Putting down at least 20% helps you avoid being underwater on your loan and reduces your monthly payments significantly.

📅 4-Year Maximum Term

Limiting your loan to 4 years or less helps you avoid paying excessive interest and ensures you're not stuck with an old car payment.

📊 10% Income Rule

Your total monthly vehicle expenses (payment, insurance, gas, maintenance) should not exceed 10% of your gross monthly income.

💰 Total Cost Consideration

Remember to factor in insurance, gas, maintenance, and registration costs when determining your true car affordability.
