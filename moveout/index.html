How the Move Out Affordability Calculator Works

Our move out affordability calculator uses the 30% rule, which recommends that your rent should not exceed 30% of your take-home income. It also analyzes all your living expenses to determine if you can afford to live independently.

The calculator considers your monthly income, rent, utilities, groceries, transportation, and other expenses to provide you with a comprehensive analysis of your financial readiness to move out.

💡 The 30% Rule

Your rent should not exceed 30% of your take-home income to ensure you have enough money for other essential expenses.

💰 Emergency Fund

Before moving out, save at least 3-6 months of expenses as an emergency fund to handle unexpected costs.

📊 Total Living Costs

Consider all expenses: rent, utilities, groceries, transportation, insurance, entertainment, and savings.

📈 Income Stability

Ensure you have stable income for at least 6 months before committing to independent living arrangements.
